## What this page is

This page explains non-specific chronic low back pain as a broad consumer-education topic: low back pain lasting long enough to affect life, without this page identifying a single structural cause. [NICE][nice-low-back-pain-sciatica]

## What this page is not

This page does not diagnose the reader, choose a care level, or provide an individualized care plan.

## Red flags: when to stop reading and seek care

Read [the red-flags reference](../RED-FLAGS.md) before using GymPrimer content for pain-related decisions.

## Plain-language overview

Low back pain is common. Many cases are managed with education, staying active when appropriate, and gradually returning to normal movement, but individual assessment matters when symptoms are severe, unusual, or persistent. [NHS][nhs-back-pain]

## What mainstream sources generally agree on

Guidelines commonly support education and exercise-based approaches for many people with low back pain. NICE includes exercise and self-management as parts of care, and the American College of Physicians recommends non-drug options such as exercise for chronic low back pain. [NICE][nice-low-back-pain-sciatica] [ACP][acp-low-back-pain-guideline]

## What is uncertain or mixed

No single exercise category is the universal answer for every back-pain presentation. Different people may respond to different approaches, and some symptoms need professional assessment before training decisions are made. [Mayo Clinic][mayo-back-pain-when-to-see-doctor]

## Commonly recommended self-management themes

Common themes include staying as active as tolerable, building general strength, using gradual exposure to movement, and avoiding sudden jumps in workload. [NHS Inform][nhs-inform-back-problems]

GymPrimer can explain movements and general principles, but it does not select an individual pain-management path.

## What to avoid

Avoid using this page to explain new severe symptoms, nerve symptoms, bladder or bowel changes, fever, trauma, or other red-flag situations. Those belong outside GymPrimer.

## When to see a professional

Use a clinician or physical therapist when symptoms are persistent, spreading, severe, paired with numbness or weakness, or unclear enough that training decisions feel uncertain. [Mayo Clinic][mayo-back-pain-when-to-see-doctor]
