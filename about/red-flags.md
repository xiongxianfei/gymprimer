# Red Flags

> Disclaimer: GymPrimer is educational content. It is not medical advice and not personalized coaching.

## What this page is

This page is a general safety-routing reference for GymPrimer pages that mention pain, posture patterns, or common conditions. It summarizes examples of symptoms that mainstream patient-education sources route to urgent or professional care. [NHS][nhs-back-pain]

## What this page is not

This page does not tell you what condition you have, choose a care level for you, or replace a clinician's assessment. It is a reference for when GymPrimer content is the wrong tool. [Mayo Clinic][mayo-back-pain-when-to-see-doctor]

## Emergency care now

Use emergency care for back pain after a major accident or fall. [MedlinePlus][medlineplus-low-back-pain-acute]

Use emergency care for back pain with new bladder or bowel control changes, numbness around the genitals or buttocks, or weakness in both legs. [NHS][nhs-back-pain]

Use emergency care for back pain with fever, major abdominal symptoms, or unexplained weight loss when a patient-education source lists those as urgent medical concerns. [Mayo Clinic][mayo-back-pain-when-to-see-doctor]

## Prompt medical care

Contact a healthcare professional for pain that is constant, intense, spreading below the knee, paired with tingling or numbness, or not improving after a period of self-care. [Mayo Clinic][mayo-back-pain-when-to-see-doctor]

Contact a healthcare professional for back pain with a history of cancer, urinary symptoms, night pain, or other concerning symptoms listed by patient-education sources. [MedlinePlus][medlineplus-low-back-pain-acute]

## Professional assessment

Use a qualified professional for individual assessment when symptoms limit daily activity, keep recurring, or make training decisions unclear. [NHS Inform][nhs-inform-back-problems]

For GymPrimer pages, "see a professional" usually means a clinician or physical therapist who can examine the person, ask follow-up questions, and account for medical history.
