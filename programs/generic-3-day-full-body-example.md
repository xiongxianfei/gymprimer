## What this page is

This page is a static worked example showing how general training principles can fit into a week for healthy beginners. [CDC][cdc-adult-activity]

## What this page is not

This is not your prescription and does not adapt to symptoms, goals, equipment, injury, medical history, or training response. [ACSM][acsm-resistance-training]

## Example week

| Day | Example focus |
| --- | --- |
| Monday | Squat pattern, horizontal push, horizontal pull, core |
| Wednesday | Hinge pattern, vertical pull or assisted pull, vertical push, carry |
| Friday | Squat or lunge pattern, horizontal push, row, core |

Each session can use simple beginner movements and moderate effort. Keep the example static: it illustrates structure, not a decision about what the reader should personally do.

## Why it is arranged this way

The example spreads practice across the week, repeats major movement patterns, and leaves non-lifting days between strength sessions. Public-health guidance supports muscle-strengthening activity on two or more days each week, and beginner program examples often use repeated full-body sessions. [CDC][cdc-adult-activity] [Fitness Wiki][rfitness-basic-beginner-routine]

## Scope boundaries

Do not use this page for pain, injury, medical context, return-to-training decisions, or individualized loading. Those require individual assessment.
