## What this page is

This page explains general weekly training frequency for healthy adults learning to train. [CDC][cdc-adult-activity]

## What this page is not

This is not your personal program and does not adapt to symptoms, goals, equipment, medical history, or training response. [ACSM][acsm-resistance-training]

## Plain-language overview

A practical beginner target is two or three strength-training days each week, with rest or easier activity between hard sessions. CDC guidance says adults should do muscle-strengthening activity on two or more days each week. [CDC][cdc-adult-activity]

## General ranges

For general fitness, many beginners can start with two full-body strength sessions per week and move toward three if recovery and schedule allow. ACSM emphasizes consistency and regular participation over complex programming for most adults. [ACSM][acsm-resistance-training]

Aerobic activity is separate: public-health guidance commonly targets 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity each week, plus muscle-strengthening activity on two or more days. [WHO][who-physical-activity]

## What changes this in real life

Sleep, soreness, work stress, prior training, and medical context can change what is reasonable. When those factors dominate, a qualified coach or clinician is a better tool than a static page.
